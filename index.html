I am a lecturer in mathematics at the University of Twente.

I'm interested in combinatorics. Recently, I've become fascinated by applying geometry to solve graph theory problems and generalising graph theory problems to geometry. Most of my work is on probabalistic and enumerative aspects of matroid theory.

Previously I was a post-doctoral fellow at the University of Waterloo, working with Peter Nelson. I received my PhD from Eindhoven University of Technology under supervision of Rudi Pendavingh and Remco van der Hofstad.

I am the editor-in-chief of the Matroid Union, a blog for and by the matroid theory community. Please send me an email if you are interested in writing a guest post.

Publications

You can find all my publications on my Google Scholar profile. Preprints are generally available on arXiv. Here is a list of my contributions to the Matroid Union.

Preprints

  1. Turán densities for matroid basis hypergraphswith Zach Walsh and Michael C. WigalarXiv[+]
    Let U be a uniform matroid. For all positive integers n and r with n ≥ r, what is the maximum number of bases of an n-element, rank-r matroid without U as a minor? We show that this question arises by restricting the problem of determining the Turán number of a daisy hypergraph to the family of matroid basis hypergraphs. We then answer this question for several interesting choices of U.
  2. Turán's theorem for Dowling geometrieswith Rutger Campbell and Donggyu KimarXiv[+]
    Turán's theorem is a fundamental result in extremal combinatorics that determines the maximum numbers of edges in a graph that does not contain a clique on t vertices. We consider similar problems in Dowling geometries.

Peer-reviewed publications

  1. An entropy argument for the number of matroidswith Nikhil Bansal and Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    J. Comb. Th. B 109 (2014)We use an entropy argument to obtain an almost tight bound on the number of matroids.
  2. On the number of matroidswith Nikhil Bansal and Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    Combinatorica 35(3), (2015)We give an asymptotic estimate for the number of matroids on a ground set of size n. Our method applies the container method.
  3. Counting matroids in minor-closed classeswith Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    J. Comb. Th. B 111 (2015)We develop the notion of cover complexity of a matroid and use it to show that almost every matroid contains a long line minor, as well as the matroids U(3,6), P6, Q6, and R6.
  4. On the number of matroids compared to the number of sparse-paving matroidswith Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    Elec. J. Comb 22(2) P2.51 (2015)We show that, on the logarithmic scale, the number of matroids is dominated by the number of sparse paving matroids.
  5. Enumerating matroids of fixed rankwith Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    Elec. J. Comb 24(1) P1.8 (2017)We consider the number of matroids of fixed rank as the size of the ground set tends to infinity.
  6. On the number of bases of almost all matroidswith Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    Combinatorica 38(4), p. 955–985 (2018)We give sharp bounds on the fraction of bases of almost all matroids. We obtain as a consequence that almost all matroids (i) contain large uniform minors (ii) are highly connected (iii) have high girth and (iv) do not arise as the truncation of another matroid.
  7. Doubly exponentially many Ingleton matroidswith Peter NelsonarXivjournal[+]
    SIAM J. Disc. Math. 32(2) (2018)A matroid is Ingleton if all quadruples of subsets of its ground set satisfy Ingleton's inequality. In particular, representable matroids are Ingleton. We show that the number of Ingleton matroids on ground set [n] is doubly exponential in n; it follows that almost all Ingleton matroids are non-representable.
  8. Asymptotics of symmetry in matroidswith Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    J. Comb. Th. B 135, p. 349–365 (2019)We prove that asymptotically almost all matroids have a trivial automorphism group, or an automorphism group generated by a single transposition. Additionally, we show that asymptotically almost all sparse paving matroids have a trivial automorphism group.
  9. On the number of biased graphswith Peter NelsonarXivjournal[+]
    SIAM J. Disc. Math. 33(1), p. 373–382 (2019)A biased graph is a graph G, together with a distinguished subset B of its cycles so that no Theta-subgraph of contains precisely two cycles in B. A large number of biased graphs can be constructed by choosing G to be a complete graph, and B to be an arbitrary subset of its Hamilton cycles.
  10. The number of partial Steiner systems and d-partitionswith Remco van der Hofstad and Rudi PendavingharXivjournal[+]
    Advances in Combinatorics (2022)We prove asymptotic upper bounds on the number of d-partitions (paving matroids of fixed rank) and partial Steiner systems (sparse paving matroids of fixed rank), using a mixture of entropy counting, sparse encoding, and the probabilistic method.
  11. Enumeration of extensions of the cycle matroid of a complete graphwith Peter Nelson and Shayla RedlinarXivjournal[+]
    Adv. Appl. Math. 138 (2022)We asymptotically count the number of single-element extensions of the complete-graphic matroid.
  12. Almost every matroid has an M(K4)- or a W3-minorarXivjournal[+]
    Elec. J. Comb. 30(4), P4.23 (2023)We show that almost every matroid contains the rank-3 whirl W3 or the complete-graphic matroid M(K4) as a minor.
  13. Tuza's conjecture for binary geometrieswith Kazuhiro NomotoarXivjournal[+]
    SIAM J. on Disc. Math. 38(2), p. 1676–1685 (2024)Tuza's conjecture states that a graph G can be made triangle-free by removing at most twice the maximum number of disjoint triangles of G. We consider a geometric version of this conjecture.
